## Epochs — consolidated rule-fidelity audit (138 divergences)

### Read this first: two systemic caveats that reclassify a large share of the list

1. **Many "divergences" pit the audited rule-text against the engine's *own* declared source of truth, not against a bug.** The combat module header says it is built to *"authentic AH 1993 — docs/AUTHENTIC-RULES.md §5"*; `scoring.ts` cites *"SPEC §9.1 / original"*; `areaValues.ts` says its numbers were *"AUTHENTIC data transcribed from the physical board's Victory Point Table (owner's scan, 2026-06-27) — replaces the earlier partly-invented values."* So the additive-combat clauses (COMBAT-02/03/04/15), the per-land scoring clauses (SCORING-02/05/06/07/08), and the area-value "off-by-one" clauses (SETUP-04/05, SCORING-08/11) are the engine deliberately following a board scan / a SPEC, while the audited rulebook extraction disagrees. Where the rule-text is *vague* ("around Epoch V") and the code is sourced from a dated scan, **the rule-text is the less reliable side** — treat those as "verify the scan," not "fix the code."

2. **A cluster of clauses are almost certainly mis-extracted or non-game rules** and should be dropped, not fixed: the publisher-correspondence block (OTHER-01…05 — "answered by mail not phone"), the "instant win for holding two regions of a former empire" block (VICTORY-01/02/03, COMBAT-02-Persian — History of the World has no instant-win), and the "capital links two lands" premise (COMBAT-06, SCORING-03). I flag these as **spurious** below rather than as defects.

## Game-material (changes strategy or outcomes)

### G1 — Turn order & draft order use a strength catch-up, not card-number / VP standing
*(DRAFT-01, DRAFT-02, DRAFT-03, DRAFT-04, TURN-02, BUILD-06, ALLOC-01, ALLOC-04, ALLOC-05, ALLOC-06, OTHER-01-strength)*
- **Rule:** Within an epoch, empires play in their printed card-number order (1→7). Drafting/allocation order is set by current Victory-Point-track standing (lowest VP picks/plays first), with strength only a tiebreak.
- **Code:** `drawOrder()` (game.ts:704-722) sorts by **cumulative drafted Empire Strength ascending** (catch-up), then VP *highest-first* (inverted), then *previous* epoch's card order. Intra-epoch turns run in **player draw order**, never by the current empires' printed `order` field (game.ts:332-338). `strengthPoints` is a monotonic all-time accumulator (game.ts:337) — a long-dead empire still skews order forever; it is never recomputed from board-held empires.
- **Fix direction:** Sequence the current epoch's empires by their `EmpireCard.order`. Derive allocation order from VP standing (lowest first) with strength/card-number as documented tiebreaks. Decide deliberately whether the strength catch-up is a *designed* substitute (then update SPEC) or a bug — right now it silently replaces two distinct rulebook orderings.

### G2 — Combat resolution is a different system than the rulebook describes
*(COMBAT-02, COMBAT-03, COMBAT-04, COMBAT-15)*
- **Rule (as extracted):** each side rolls one die; attacker adds its army count, defender adds its army count + terrain value; higher total wins.
- **Code:** keep-highest-of-k d6 (attacker 2 dice / 3 with Leader; defender 1 die / 2 in difficult terrain), one-army-per-round multi-round, flat +1 for fort, ties rerolled (combat.ts:44-155).
- **Skeptic's note:** the code header explicitly claims this *is* the authentic AH 1993 model, and real History of the World does use the army-count-additive die system — so **one of the two is wrong about "authentic."** This single decision dominates every battle's odds; resolve it before anything else. Either (a) the extracted clauses are right and the engine needs the additive model, or (b) AUTHENTIC-RULES.md is right and these clauses should be struck. Until reconciled, treat as the highest-leverage open question, not a mechanical one-line fix.

### G3 — Forts are far weaker than the rule's "raise-to-value" fort
*(COMBAT-04-terrain-step, COMBAT-01/02/03/04/05-fort, COMBAT-08-tie)*
- **Rule:** a fort has a printed value (e.g. 9) that *raises* the defender's die result to that value (a floor, not an add), applies on the first round only, and is removed once the defending combat resolves; alternatively modeled as a +1 terrain-category step (extra die).
- **Code:** flat `+1` to the defender's kept die **every round** (combat.ts:55,131; game.ts comment confirms "+1 EVERY round"), removed only on a *successful conquest* (game.ts:981) — a fort that *wins* its defense persists. No printed value, no first-round limit, no raise-to-value.
- **Fix direction:** if the "value-9 floor" reading is correct, this is a large strategic gap (a true fort is near-impregnable for a round); add a `value` to the fort `StructureKind`, make it a `max(roll, value)` floor on round 1 only, and remove the fort after the combat regardless of outcome.

### G4 — Capitals confer no defensive bonus
*(COMBAT-06-capital, COMBAT-07, COMBAT-08-cap+fort, COMBAT-06-capitalland)*
- **Rule:** defending in a Capital land raises terrain difficulty by one category (capped at one step even with a fort also present).
- **Code:** `combatContext` (game.ts:919-940) inspects only `difficultTerrain` and `fort`; a capital piece grants **zero** combat advantage. The capital+fort cap is moot because neither half exists.
- **Fix direction:** give capitals a defensive modifier (extra defender die or +1) in `combatContext`, and cap the combined capital+fort effect at one step.

### G5 — Monument building: forced, wrong cost, wrong site, wrong "controls" check
*(MONUMENT-01 ×4 variants, MONUMENT-02, MONUMENT-03 ×2, MONUMENT-05, MONUMENT-09, MONUMENTS-01/02)*
- **Rule:** an active empire *may* (optional) build a Monument **in a resource land it controls**; cost is one resource per build; a monument needs the land it sits on, and an empire cannot score a monument whose land it no longer controls.
- **Code:** `buildMonuments` (game.ts:836-851) **forces** `floor(resourceLands/2)` monuments — two resource lands per monument, no opt-out. `monumentSites()` (game.ts:1013-1018) forces a tier order **Capital → City → resource land**, so monuments land on resource-*less* capitals/cities first; the resource requirement only gates the lowest fallback tier. Scoring counts monuments purely by `owner` with **no land-control check** (scoring.ts:161-166), so a monument whose defending army was wiped by an enemy Plague keeps scoring for its old owner. On capture, monuments **transfer** to the conqueror (game.ts:209-210) rather than the rulebook's remove-on-loss.
- **Fix direction:** make construction optional and player-chosen; cost = controlled resource lands (1 per resource, not per pair); restrict sites to resource lands the empire controls; in scoring, require the owner to still hold an army on the monument's land (or re-clear `owner` when the land is vacated/captured).

### G6 — Fleets persist forever, cross from coasts you don't hold, and skip ocean combat
*(FLEETS-01/03/04, RETREAT-01/04, FLEET-03, STACK-03/04, EXPANSION-07, COMBAT-09/10/11, SCORING-04/06-sea)*
- **Rule:** a fleet only ferries armies from a coastal land you hold to another land on the same sea; it must base in a friendly adjacent land or is lost; sea/ocean stacking is limited (ocean = sea + 1); naval combat uses the terrain system and obeys combat events; a controlled sea scores its **printed** value, and only seas adjacent to an ocean score.
- **Code:** fleets are pushed into `state.fleets` and **persist across all epochs/empires** with no basing maintenance (game.ts:1094-1098; RETREAT-04 unmodeled). `computeFrontier` lets a player land armies on *any* coast of a navigable sea they have a leftover fleet in **even with no coastal land there** (game.ts:887-893) — a real exploit once a new empire inherits old fleets. Stacking is hardcoded `< 2` for **both** seas and oceans (game.ts:1074,1096) — no ocean +1, not "any number." Ocean combat is skipped entirely (`enemyFleets=0` when `isOcean`, game.ts:1081); naval combat uses a fixed `NAVAL_CTX` that ignores terrain and all turn effects (game.ts:42-50,1087). Sea VP is a flat +1 per controlled non-ocean sea (game.ts:1057-1061; scoring.ts:170) with no per-sea value and no ocean-adjacency gate.
- **Fix direction:** enforce that crossing requires currently holding a coastal source land (check at crossing, not just placement); add fleet basing maintenance (lose unbased fleets); make ocean cap = sea cap + 1; resolve ocean combat; pass `TurnEffects` into naval combat; if seas have printed values, store and score them, and add the ocean-adjacency relation.

### G7 — Empire special abilities are entirely absent
*(EVENT-01 Carthage, EVENT-02 Vedic, EVENT-03/04 Persia, MONUMENT-04/05 Babylon, SCORING-03-dominance-region)*
- **Rule:** specific empires carry printed powers — Carthage/Vedic protect their capital, Persia double-scores one held square, Babylon's monument needs Capital+2 resources and scores only while it holds Babylon, plus empire-card dominance-region bonuses.
- **Code:** `EmpireCard` (types.ts:47-60) has **no ability field**; every empire is generic. None of these powers exist.
- **Fix direction:** add an `ability`/`special` field to `EmpireCard` and wire the handful of powered empires through the event/scoring/combat phases. This is a whole feature layer, not a patch — scope it deliberately.

### G8 — Vacant/disaster events don't vacate, ignore the city-exclusion, and aren't unified
*(EVENTS-15/16/17, RETREAT-06/07)*
- **Rule:** a Vacant Event clears **one chosen Area** of everything (city, capital, monument, units), and **cannot target an Area containing a City**. Rebellion/civil-war/revolt events exist as a separate mechanic.
- **Code:** plague/pestilence/famine (game.ts:581-621) are **probabilistic per-army d6 kills** that rarely empty an Area; structure disasters are a *separate* effect razing one land's structures. `disasterTargets` (637-660) has **no city-exclusion guard**. No rebellion/civil-war/revolt effect kind exists at all.
- **Fix direction:** if Vacant Events should deterministically clear a whole Area, add that effect kind with the city-exclusion guard; decide whether the probabilistic-plague behavior is a deliberate redesign (document it) or replace it.

### G9 — Minor-Empire and Unit-Event flexibility removed
*(EVENTS-04 flexible-SP, EVENTS-06/07/08/09 unit events, EVENTS-11 free turn slot, EVENTS-14 expansion-order choice, EVENTS-05 SP-on-track)*
- **Rule:** a Minor Empire has no fixed turn slot (player chooses when to play it) and the player chooses whether the main or minor empire expands first; a Unit-Event unit must be placed next to a controlled Capital, can't *start* a new expansion but can extend one, and a lone Unit-Event unit counts as a navigation expansion; a single event SP can flexibly modify one battle or reinforce a prior Minor Empire.
- **Code:** the minor empire always runs immediately before the main empire in the same `playEmpireTurnGen`, main-first order hardcoded (game.ts:354-363) — no player choice of slot or order. Bonus armies merge into the shared `remaining` pool and place via `computeFrontier` with no next-to-Capital constraint and no new-expansion restriction (game.ts:405,685-687). Flexible single-SP and SP-on-VP-track are unmodeled.
- **Fix direction:** offer the player/bot a choice of minor-empire slot and expansion order; constrain Unit-Event placement to a controlled-Capital frontier. Lower priority than G1–G6 but does affect optimal play.

### G10 — Three-player variant entirely unimplemented
*(VICTORY-02/03/04-3player)*
- **Rule:** in a 3-player game each player takes two turns per epoch and draws two empires that persist across those turns (stronger one played second).
- **Code:** the turn loop plays exactly one empire per player per epoch (game.ts:332-338); no player-count branching anywhere.
- **Fix direction:** **material only if 3-player is a supported mode.** If Epochs is fixed to its current player set, mark N/A in SPEC; otherwise this is a whole mode to build.

### G11 — Area-value table errors (verify against the scan)
*(SETUP-04, SETUP-05, SCORING-08-india, SCORING-11-the-4)*
- **Rule (extracted):** Eurasia/N. America first score in Epoch IV; S. America/Africa/Nippon "around Epoch V"; India = 2 in Epoch I; the single "4" is South-East Asia in Epoch VII.
- **Code (confirmed in `areaValues.ts`):** Eurasia/N. America first score at index 4 = **Epoch V**; the three southern areas at index 5 = **Epoch VI**; `india[0] = 1`; the single 4 is `northern_europe[6]`, **not** South-East Asia.
- **Skeptic's note:** the table header says it was transcribed from the owner's physical board scan on 2026-06-27, explicitly replacing "partly-invented" values. So these are either genuine transcription slips **or** the extracted rule-text is wrong. The crisp ones (India=1-vs-2, the 4's region) are worth a 30-second re-check against the scan image; the vague "around Epoch V" ones are not actionable. **Do not blindly "fix" the table to match the rule-text — confirm which source is authoritative first.**

## Cosmetic / edge / non-applicable

- **Pure representation, behaviorally equivalent:** SETUP-02 (VP track is an integer, not an on-board marker), SETUP-06 (empires in per-epoch piles vs one combined face-down deck), SETUP-01 (Sumeria seeded as 4 armies, not a "white card in the board center" — and the 9-piles-of-7 structure actually *matches*), EXPANSION-04 (units placed one-at-a-time with no off-board purchase pool — end-state identical), BUILD-07-forts (forts are build-time placements; only the city half diverges → folds into G5).
- **Timing/secrecy in a vs-AI game:** DRAFT-05/08/09 (draft secrecy, can't-inspect-passed-card, no table-talk), EVENTS-02/04 (declare "during" cards up-front rather than "at any point"; no simultaneous secret study). Effects still resolve correctly; only the information-hiding choreography is absent. Minor at best against an AI.
- **Ties:** COMBAT-08 — engine rerolls ties; rule says "defender wins ties." Net outcome (attacker not placed on a tie) is the same; only Fanaticism's tie-flip differs. Edge.
- **Implemented-but-dead content:** the strait and Great Wall terrain mechanics exist in `combat.ts`/`combatContext` but **no board land is tagged** with them (only the test fixture is) — COMBAT-05/06-terrain, COMBAT-08/09-border, SEAS-01/03/04/06, FLEETS-01-strait-bridge. These are a **content gap, not a logic bug**: add the tags to `board.ts` (and the fleet-bridges-strait exemption) if those features are intended, otherwise the code is harmless dead weight.
- **Optional advanced rules (lower priority by design):** EXPANSION-01 (Preservation of Culture as an optional toggle — currently always-on with no toggle), EXPANSION-03 (Fortress = upgrade two spent SP; engine charges 1 SP for a fort), EXPANSION-05 (Empires Expand). EXPANSION-02 (Preservation-of-Culture *combat* bonus from monuments) is the one genuinely missing mechanic here and could fold into G7 if those optional rules are in scope.
- **Component minutiae / unlikely-real:** FLEET-02 (two fleet symbols on one piece), MONUMENT-06 (monument marked on VP track by color), EVENTS-18/SETUP-01 ("six suits" — the audit itself notes nine-piles-of-seven is the real structure, so "six suits" is the suspect side).

## Skeptical: likely mis-extracted rules — do NOT implement without source confirmation

- **OTHER-01…05** — publisher mail/phone correspondence policy. Not game logic. Drop from the audit.
- **VICTORY-01/02/03 + COMBAT-02-Persian** — "attacking while holding two regions of a former empire wins instantly." History of the World has **no instant-win**; the only win is highest cumulative VP after Epoch VII (game.ts:854-864). Almost certainly a bad extraction; do not build.
- **COMBAT-06-capital-links / SCORING-03-linked-lands** — "a capital links two lands." Capitals are single-land everywhere in real HotW and in this engine. Likely a misread of the capital icon. The *real* capital gap is G4 (defensive bonus), not land-linking.

## Bottom line on the count

Of the 138 entries, after collapsing duplicate IDs and removing the spurious/N-A/worked-example buckets, the actionable picture is roughly: **~10 game-material clusters (G1–G11)**, a dozen cosmetic/representation items, a content-gap (straits/Great Wall) that is data not code, and ~20 entries that are either the engine correctly following its own SPEC/scan against unreliable rule-text or outright mis-extracted rules. The two that most distort actual play are the **turn/draft ordering (G1)** and the **combat/fort math (G2/G3/G4)** — and both hinge on first settling *which* document (the extracted rulebook vs. SPEC/AUTHENTIC-RULES.md) is canonical.

### Top fixes (ranked)
1. RECONCILE COMBAT MODEL FIRST (G2): decide whether the army-count-additive die system (extracted rulebook) or the keep-highest-dice model (combat.ts, claims 'authentic AH 1993') is canonical. This one decision moves every battle's odds and gates G3/G4 — settle the source-of-truth before writing code.
2. Fix turn & draft ordering (G1): sequence the current epoch's empires by EmpireCard.order, and derive allocation order from VP standing (lowest first) instead of the cumulative-drafted-strength catch-up; also recompute 'empire strength' from board-held empires rather than the monotonic all-time accumulator (game.ts:704-722,332-338,337).
3. Repair monument scoring/control (G5): require the owner to still hold an army on a monument's land before it scores (scoring.ts:161-166), so an enemy plague that vacates the land stops the old owner scoring it; and make monument loss-on-capture match the rule instead of transferring ownership (game.ts:209-210).
4. Close the fleet exploits (G6): require a currently-held coastal source land at crossing time, not just at placement (computeFrontier game.ts:887-893); add fleet basing maintenance so unbased fleets are lost (RETREAT-04); these persistent-fleet gaps are directly abusable across epochs.
5. Give forts the rulebook's strength (G3): add a printed fort value with a max(roll,value) floor on round one only, remove the fort after the combat regardless of outcome, instead of the flat +1-every-round/remove-only-on-conquest behavior (combat.ts:55,131; game.ts:981).
6. Add a capital defensive bonus (G4): wire capitals into combatContext (game.ts:919-940) as an extra defender die / +1, capped at one step when a fort is also present.
7. Correct fleet/sea stacking and ocean combat (G6): make ocean capacity = sea capacity + 1 (both are hardcoded <2, game.ts:1074,1096), resolve ocean combat instead of skipping it (game.ts:1081), and pass TurnEffects into naval combat (game.ts:1087) so combat events apply at sea.
8. Fix monument build cost/site selection (G5): make construction optional and player-chosen, cost one-per-controlled-resource-land (not floor(resourceLands/2)), and restrict sites to controlled resource lands rather than the Capital->City->resource tier-forcing (game.ts:836-851,1013-1018).
9. Verify the area-value table against the 2026-06-27 board scan (G11) for the crisp discrepancies only — India Epoch I (1 vs 2) and the single '4' (northern_europe vs South-East Asia) — and fix whichever of table-or-rule-text is actually wrong; ignore the vague 'around Epoch V' clauses.
10. Add empire special abilities (G7): introduce an ability field on EmpireCard (types.ts:47-60) and implement the powered empires (Carthage/Vedic capital protection, Persia double-square, Babylon's conditional monument) — scope as a feature, not a patch.
11. Restore Minor-Empire / Unit-Event flexibility (G9): let the player/bot choose the minor empire's turn slot and main-vs-minor expansion order (game.ts:354-363), and constrain Unit-Event placement to a controlled-Capital frontier.
12. Decide and document the spurious/optional/3-player items: strike the mail-correspondence and instant-win clauses from the audit, mark the 3-player variant (G10) and optional rules (Preservation-of-Culture combat bonus, Fortress upgrade) as N/A-or-backlog in SPEC, and tag straits/Great Wall (G content gap) as 'add board data or remove dead paths'.
