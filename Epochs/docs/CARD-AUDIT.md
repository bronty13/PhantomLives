# Epochs — Empire-Card Roster Audit

> Generated by the `card-audit` multi-agent workflow (17 agents): each Epoch's two
> physical empire-card photos were read and the authoritative roster (strength, start
> land, navigation, capital, card #) diffed against our generated `empires.ts`. The
> copyrighted card scans live only in `~/Downloads/HOTW` (gitignored) — this file holds
> the uncopyrightable game **data** (numbers, place names) and our own-words notes.

**52 discrepancies (40 game-material).** Our roster was research-derived and had drifted
from the cards: ~24 wrong start-lands (several colliding with another empire's seat),
9 navigation errors, turn-order swaps, a wrong strength, and the **entire Inca empire
missing**. The cards confirmed there are **no per-card "special ability" paragraphs** —
empire asymmetry is the numbers (strength, capital, navigation); the handful of true
special-rules are Event cards + two rulebook footnotes.

## Applied (v0.46.0)
- **Inca added** (Epoch VI) — strength 2, Northern Andes, capital; it shares the
  New-World card with the Aztec, so Epoch VI now carries 8 empires. **Aztec strength
  4 → 2** to match the paired card.
- **~24 start-lands corrected** to the printed homelands (all target territories already
  existed): e.g. Babylonia → Middle Tigris, Indus Valley → Lower Indus, Sassanids →
  Zagros, Chou → Wei River, Romans → Southern Apennines, Macedonia → Pindus, Han →
  Great Plain, Ottomans → Western Anatolia, Spain → Pyrenees, Portugal → Western Iberia,
  Russia → North European Plain, USA → Appalachia, Germany → Baltic Seaboard, …
- **Navigation corrected** to the cards: Han gains its Pacific seas (it had none!),
  Romans + Persia + Macedonia gain Red/Black/North seas, Carthaginia loses its invented
  Atlantic, Sassanids lose their invented Persian Gulf.
- **Turn order (card #) fixed**: the Epoch-I +1 offset (Sumeria's removed slot), and the
  Assyria↔Greek (Epoch II) and Guptas↔Byzantines (Epoch IV) swaps.

## Deferred (deliberate)
- **Aztec/Inca shared-card mechanic** — modelled as two separate draftable empires for
  now (gets Inca on the board); the authentic "one card, one player, two homelands"
  rule is a future special-rule.
- **Goths' start land "Danubia"** — no matching territory on our 100-land map; left at
  Western Steppe pending a Danube-basin territory.
- **The Aegean sea-model** — our finer map splits the Mediterranean into med + Aegean +
  Adriatic, so some "extra aegean_sea" diffs are our model correctly including the Aegean
  in the cards' single "E. Med", not errors. Kept as-is.
- **`T'ang Dynasty` card #9** — a likely scan misread (>7 in a 7-empire epoch); left.

All start-lands + nav seas validated by `build-data.mjs` (no warnings); 138 tests pass;
difficulty ladder steady.
