**Subject:** Your calendar app — now easier to open, with some nice new additions

Hi Jan,

I've made some lovely improvements to your CalendarMaker, and changed one thing
that makes life easier for both of us.

**The one change to make:** instead of opening the file I copied onto your laptop,
you'll now open it from a **web link**. It works the same, but it keeps itself up
to date automatically — you'll never have to copy a file again, and you'll always
have the latest version.

**Setting it up (just once):**

1. Click this link — it opens in your web browser:
   https://bronty13.github.io/calendarmaker/

2. Save it so it's easy to find next time. Near the top of the browser, at the
   right end of the address bar, there's a small **star** ☆. Click the star, then
   click **Done**. That saves a bookmark.

3. From now on, open your calendar from that bookmark. That's all.

**What's new since you last used it:**

- **Bible verses and sayings on any day.** Click a day and add a verse or a saying,
  right alongside your reminders and birthdays.

- **A quick way to find a verse.** Type a reference like "John 3:16" and press
  Enter — or click your way through: pick the book, then the chapter, then the
  verse. Books show in short form (like "Gen" for Genesis) so they all fit; rest
  your mouse on one to see the full name.

- **Morning Affirmations are built in.** When you add a saying, you'll find a set
  of morning affirmations ready to choose from.

- **Two ways verses and sayings can print:** inside each day's box, or on their own
  separate page — whichever you like (switch with the two buttons at the top of a
  calendar).

- **Add your own sayings more easily** in Settings — add, edit, or delete them any
  time.

- **A "Help" button** at the top opens a simple, complete guide, and you can make
  its text bigger with the **A+** button inside it.

- **It updates itself.** When something new is ready, a **green bar** appears at the
  top — just click **Update now**. Your calendars are never lost.

**One handy tip:** to make everything on the screen bigger, hold the **Ctrl** key
and press the **+** key (do it again for bigger still).

**Could you try this for me?**

1. Click the link above and bookmark it.
2. Click **+ New calendar**, give it any name, and click **Create**.
3. Click a day and add a little reminder — and try adding a Bible verse too.

Then just let me know it worked, or send me a screenshot if anything looks odd.

If you made calendars in the old version that you'd like to keep, don't worry —
tell me and we'll move them over together. It's quick. (For the helper: see
`docs/migration-checklist.md`.)
